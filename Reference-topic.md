## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|----------|----------|
| Calendar is not visible | Restart Microsoft Teams and sign in again. |
| Invitation was not sent | Check the attendee email addresses. |
| Date or time is incorrect | Edit the meeting and update the schedule. |
| Unable to create a meeting | Verify that your account has meeting permissions. |

## Meeting Fields

When scheduling a meeting in Microsoft Teams, users must complete several fields to define the meeting details. The following describes the purpose of each field.

- Title: Name of the meeting
- Required Attendees: Participants who must attend
- Optional Attendees: Participants who may attend
- Date: Meeting date
- Start Time: Meeting start time
- End Time: Meeting end time
- Description: Meeting agenda or notes
- Recurrence: Repeating meeting schedule

## Meeting Types

Microsoft Teams supports different types of meetings to meet various collaboration requirements. Choosing the appropriate meeting type helps organizers manage participants and meeting schedules effectively.

| Meeting Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| One-time Meeting | Scheduled for a single occurrence |
| Recurring Meeting | Repeats on a defined schedule |
| Channel Meeting | Conducted within a Teams channel |
| Private Meeting | Invitation-only meeting |

## Notes

- Microsoft Teams automatically creates a meeting link for every scheduled online meeting.
- Meeting invitations are sent immediately after the meeting is saved.
- Recurring meetings follow the schedule configured by the organizer.
- Meeting recordings are available only if recording is enabled and permitted by the organization.
